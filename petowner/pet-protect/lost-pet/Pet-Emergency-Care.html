Pet Poison Myths

MYTH NOTES
"Fresh Packet" is harmless FALSE: Products act as dessicants to control spoilage in packaged goods. The ingredients commonly used in these packets irritate the gastric mucosa, particularly the iron containing and color-indicator packets.
Swiffer WetJet kills dogs FALSE: Product is 80-90% water and type of alcohol used (propylene) is much less toxic than other alcohols.
ULTRA CLOROX harmful due to lye ingredient FALSE: Lye's chemical ingredient is sodium hydroxide; CLOROX uses aqueous sodium hypochlorite, which is closely related but not as harmful; CLOROX should be kept away from pets but does not contain lye.
Agent Orange in pot scrubbing sponges FALSE: Sponges have liquid anti-bacterial in the inner part of the sponge, which is non-toxic. Sponges can still be dangerous if they become saturated with grease.
